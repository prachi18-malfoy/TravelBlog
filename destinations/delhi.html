Discover Delhi

The vibrant capital of India, where history and modernity merge. Explore Delhi's majestic monuments, bustling bazaars, and rich cultural heritage.

About Delhi

Despite its less than wonderful reputation as a travel destination, to miss out on visiting Delhi during a trip to India would be a bit of a sin. There are plenty of reasons to given India’s capital a chance, from its food, to its lush parks in the south of the city, to its mish-mash of cultures and languages that represent the varied tapestry that the sub-continent of India covers. Delhi is a summary of all that India is – from its super modern metro to its crumbling havelis (historic stately homes), from its dusty spice markets to its gleaming and luxurious shopping malls. But if there’s one reason that stands out most to us as a reason to visit, it’s Delhi’s history. Delhi is one of the longest-serving capitals in the world, and has witnessed the rise and fall of empires. A walk through Delhi can take you past monuments from the late 1100s to the present day, in a city heavily influenced by the Mughals, the British and many others.

Highlights of Delhi

Some must-visit landmarks in Delhi include:

  • India Gate: A war memorial and a symbol of pride for the nation.
  • Red Fort: An iconic Mughal-era fort and a UNESCO World Heritage site.
  • Qutub Minar: A towering monument showcasing ancient Indian craftsmanship.
  • Old Delhi: You can’t visit Delhi without seeing it’s historic heart, winding lanes and the Jameh Masjid (Mosque).
  • Humayan’s Tomb.: Tomb to the Mughal Emperor of Delhi, Humayun, this tomb is a beautiful UNESCO heritage sight, and one of the best examples of Persian influenced architecture in Delh
  • Lotus Temple: A stunning piece of modern architecture dedicated to peace.

Travel Tips for Delhi

Visiting Delhi is a delightful experience if you plan well. Here are some tips:

  • Visit between October and March for the best weather.
  • Use the Delhi Metro to explore the city efficiently and affordably.
  • Try local dishes like chole bhature, parathas, and kebabs in Old Delhi.
  • Wear comfortable shoes as you will walk a lot in historical sites and bazaars.
  • Stay hydrated and carry sunscreen, especially during the summer months.
  • Just after Diwali is not the ideal time to visit Delhi.
  • If you’re staying in a mid-range or upscale hotel (which is a good idea), arrange an airport pickup directly with them for a smooth landing
  • Ignore offers of “free” tuk-tuk rides or shopping tours. Anything that is “free” will invariably lead to you being taken to a number of shops
  • The key with Delhi is to be cautious but not paranoid – hold your head up high, walk like you know where you’re going, and you’ll likely find people leave you alone!
